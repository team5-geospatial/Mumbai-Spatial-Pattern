Spatial Pattern Analysis

Discussion

This maps illustrates the temporal changes in Mumbai City's urban spatial patterns from 1990 to 2020, categorizing areas into "Scattered Settlement," "Sub Urban Fringe," "Secondary Core," and "Primary Core." Over this 30-year period, Mumbai demonstrates a dramatic and widespread urban expansion and densification. In 1990, while the southern part of the island already showed a significant "Primary Core," surrounding areas still contained considerable "Sub Urban Fringe" and "Scattered Settlement." By 2000, there's a clear expansion of both "Primary" and "Secondary Core" areas, especially northward. This trend accelerates intensely by 2010, with the "Primary Core" consolidating and encompassing a much larger portion of the island and mainland extensions. By 2020, the maps depict an almost entirely urbanized landscape, where "Primary Core" areas dominate nearly the entire region, indicating extreme urban infill and outward sprawl, with very little "Scattered Settlement" remaining, reflecting Mumbai's continuous and intense metropolitan growth.
